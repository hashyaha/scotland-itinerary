⚠️ Schedule Watch Points
🕘
Day 1 — Urquhart Castle: Go inside today — you have time. Buy tickets online (~£12) to skip the queue.
🅿️
Day 2 — Storr parking: 07:00 departure is firm. Car park fills by 9am on good days — a slow breakfast costs you the spot.
🚐
Day 3 — Single-track roads: Glen Etive and Talisker Bay are narrow. A minibus needs more space for passing places — go slow.
⏱
Day 3 — Glen Etive is a dead end: Drive in and back out the same way. Budget the full 45 min.
🚨
Day 4 — 12:30 is non-negotiable. Set a group alarm for 12:00. Agree a hike turnaround time before you start.
🌧
Bad weather cuts: Talisker Bay (Day 3, saves ~1h) → Fairy Glen (Day 2, ~40 min). Never cut: Storr, Glenfinnan, Lost Valley.
🚂
Jacobite Train: Confirm on West Coast Railways — runs from mid-May. Return crossing ≈ 15:10.
🅿️
Parking: Storr (~£5) · Kilt Rock (~£3) · Fairy Glen (~£3) · Glenfinnan (~£3.50). Mostly card, carry some cash.

👟 Footwear (critical)
  • Proper hiking shoes/boots with ankle support — non-negotiable for Lost Valley
  • Trainers fine for non-hiking days
  • Waterproof hiking boots preferred — paths are wet and rocky
🧥 Clothing layers
  • Base layer (moisture-wicking)
  • Mid-layer fleece or thick jumper
  • Genuinely waterproof shell jacket — not just shower-resistant
  • T-shirts and long sleeves
  • Beanie hat + thin gloves (cold at summits even in May)
🦟 Midge protection
  • Smidge or Avon Skin So Soft — most recommended in Scotland
  • DEET-based repellent as backup
  • Midge head net — worst at Sligachan & Talisker Bay at dawn
  • Apply before leaving the minibus, not once surrounded
🎒 Day pack (group)
  • 1.5L water per person on hike days
  • High-energy snacks (no café at Lost Valley summit)
  • Basic group first aid kit
  • OS Maps or AllTrails downloaded OFFLINE — no signal in Glencoe
  • Spare dry layer in a dry bag
  • Trekking poles (optional, useful on Lost Valley descent)
  • Portable phone charger / power bank
🚐 Minibus
  • Cash (parking machines, rural cafés)
  • Bin bags for muddy kit
  • Snacks for long drive days
  • Google Maps downloaded offline for all routes
✨ Insider Tips
📸
Eilean Donan best shot: Stop at the lay-by ~200m east of the entrance on A87, not the car park. Castle + bridge + loch in one frame. Soft morning light at 07:45.
⬅️
Old Man of Storr: Take the left fork — 10 min longer but views reveal gradually. Walk all the way around the pinnacles; they look completely different from behind.
🚂
Glenfinnan viewpoint: Go left out of the car park, follow the signs uphill. Stand above and south of the viaduct — not underneath it.
🌊
Talisker Bay: Walk all the way to the far end. The waterfall dropping into the sea is the real highlight — most people stop halfway and miss it.
🗿
Sligachan Bridge legend: Dip your face in the river under the old stone bridge. 7 years of youth. The group reaction is worth it 😄
⛰
Buachaille shot: Lay-by at the A82 / Glen Etive junction. On calm days the mountain reflects in the River Coupall.
🌅
Daylight: Light until 21:30–22:00. Don't stress — golden hour on Day 3 at Buachaille will be spectacular.
🦟
Midges: Apply repellent before leaving the minibus, not once surrounded. Worst near still water at dawn/dusk.
🥾
Lost Valley: The rocky entrance squeeze is the hardest bit. The valley is 10 min beyond — worth pushing through.
🐑
Sheep on the road — they don't move for minibuses. Patience required.
📶
Phone signal: Very patchy in Glencoe and Glen Etive. Download all maps offline before arriving.
